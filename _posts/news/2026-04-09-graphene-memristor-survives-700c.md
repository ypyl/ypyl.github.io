date: 2026-04-09

---

Researchers from MIT and several other universities have developed a graphene-based memristor using a Gra/HfO2/W structure that can reliably operate at temperatures up to 700°C. This performance far exceeds that of any current commercial memory technology, opening new possibilities for AI applications in extreme environments.

This breakthrough was published in the journal Science.
