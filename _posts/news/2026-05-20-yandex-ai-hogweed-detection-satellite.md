At the **CIPR Digital-2026** conference, Yandex won the "Green Digital" award for an AI-powered project that detects **Sosnowsky's hogweed** — a toxic invasive plant — using satellite imagery. The service employs a computer vision model trained on a dataset of **10,000 images** to automatically flag infestation hotspots, completing the task roughly **50 times faster** than manual search.

The system has already helped identify large infestation zones across **18 regions of Russia** and currently monitors territories in the Tver and Yaroslavl regions covering approximately **100,000 km²**. The project highlights how computer vision and satellite data are becoming practical tools for ecology, land monitoring, and natural risk management — a reminder that AI extends well beyond chatbots and code assistants.
