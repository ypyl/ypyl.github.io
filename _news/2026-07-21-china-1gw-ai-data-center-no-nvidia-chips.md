Z.ai (formerly Zhipu AI) has completed a massive **1-gigawatt** compute facility powered entirely by Chinese-made accelerators, with no Nvidia chips, and has already partially commissioned it for training next-generation **GLM** models, according to Bloomberg. The company also operates several additional clusters of over 10,000 chips each. While this doesn't prove domestic chips match Nvidia on performance or ecosystem, it demonstrates a viable alternative compute platform is forming — as Beijing considers roughly **$295 billion** in investment for a national data-centre network requiring at least **80%** domestic technology.
