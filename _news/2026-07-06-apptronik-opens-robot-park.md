Apptronik has launched **Robot Park** in Austin, an 8,000 sq m facility designed as a continuous data-collection pipeline for humanoid robots. **Apollo 2** units in both bipedal and wheeled configurations work autonomously and via teleoperation on logistics tasks—carrying boxes, opening doors, and navigating uneven terrain—while the gathered data feeds both the design of **Apollo 3** and Google DeepMind's **Gemini Robotics** model training. With its own dedicated proving ground, Apptronik aims to overcome the industry-wide data shortage and gain an edge over competitors like 1X, Figure, and Tesla Optimus Academy in building universal robots for unadapted human environments.
